Welcome to Post Oak Press!

By: Isa Pedersen 

Post Oak Press is the Post Oak High School’s new student-run newspaper that is specifically for the high school community. We created the Post Oak Press because, let's face it, no one reads The Post. There’s rarely anything in it that concerns us. In the Post Oak Press, the entire paper is dedicated to our community. It’s a student paper, made by the students, for the students. You can expect a variety of

important information, as well as fun reads and games. Some things that will be included are: 


Staff Spotlights: dedicated to one of our wonderful Post Oak Staff members and their story.


Games: crossword puzzle, word search,  or other ways to distract yourself during OWT


Post Oak Pets: the beloved pets of our community (plants are invited as well)


Internship Column: Information about various available internships and recaps of student experiences.


Sports: Coverage of recent games, and times and dates of upcoming events.

And more!

Community Meeting! Continued.

..Though they aren’t usually this chaotic, the Post Oak High School Community Meeting is a time that’s really important to democracy in the school because it lets the students share what’s new in the community. That may entail discussions about new updates in the community, bringing up issues that might need to be discussed, or even just giving a friend, classmate, teacher, or anyone else a compliment. It may all seem normal to someone who’s been at The Post Oak High School, but that normalcy is something that another school would long to have. Being able to just bring up something that you’ve been thinking about and have it considered and discussed by the entire student body and staff as one big community is something that’s uniquely Post Oak. So how have we taken advantage of this ability we’ve been given? Over the three years I’ve been at the high school, I’ve experienced many meetings. From the huge zoom meetings during the height of Covid-19, through the slow introduction of outdoor community meetings in the school pavilion, to finally this year and late last year, indoor community meetings. Despite where we were located, the students always had the courage to debate issues in the community. Some of the top ones I can recall are discussions of issues like dirty dishes in the kitchen sink, missing silverware, stinky bathrooms, and one of the big ones that struck up many meetings of discussion, the appearance policy. The community has also talked through new decisions, like the school rules we all help to decide on every school year, and we’ve shared out-of-school events like political events and marches. Anything is welcome to be shared in a community meeting, and we aren’t afraid to utilize this unique opportunity we’ve been given. That’s why the Community Meeting at the Post Oak High School is so important to the democracy and strengthening of the community as a whole.

Odyssey!

By: Isa Pedersen

To start out the school year, we took an amazing trip to Prude Ranch near Ft. Davis, a charming ranch in the heart of beautiful West Texas. The point of our Odyssey trips is to bond with the community and learn about new people while experiencing amazing things together. While in West Texas we got the chance to learn about and actively participate in a variety of activities including, horseback riding, compass orienteering, Meca Art Galleries, geology and rocks, and some beautiful hikes through Fort Davis, the Chihuahua Desert, and the Madera Canyon.

Through some of these experiences, like horseback riding and compass orienteering, we talked to and got to know some of the locals of Prude Ranch. Claire Thacker stated “These activites helped us connect with others and learn more about each other in the process.” One horse activity was a little more hands-on. We first took a two hour long ride through the hills of Prude Ranch, and then came back to learn how to tack a horse, clean them, and even bond with them, as well. The other activity where we got to interact with a Prude Ranch local was in Compass Orienteering. In Compass Orienteering we learned how to use a compass and even learned about the sport, compass orienteering. In the end, not everyone could make the compass accurate enough, but we all got candy so the majority of us were happy! The other favorite activities were the beautiful hikes around West Texas.

A fan favorite location was the trail through the Chihuahua desert. In this trail, we scaled down a canyon and got to learn all about the rock formations, the erosion, and the drought that has been going on in Texas. This was the most advanced hike we did, making it the most fun. One student even lost their water bottle on this hike! The community definitely bonded on this trip, whether it was during the amazing activities we did, while sleeping in our assigned cabins, or over discussing whether or not the food was appetizing. Thank you again to Ms. Novak and Mr. Roddy for making this a trip none of us will forget!

Sports!

By: Amelie Pedersen

This semester several sports teams have started their seasons–volleyball, cross country, and soccer club. The volleyball team usually plays one to two times a week, and Allie Dunaway was more than ready to start: “I’m so excited to have represented Post Oak… during our first volleyball game of the season.” The team has already won five games! The soccer club started on Monday, September 12, and they practice every Monday and Wednesday at the pavilion on the South Campus. Cross country runs every Monday and Wednesday morning from 6:30 to 7:30 with Mr. Jacobs. One of our cross-country teammates Natalie Gross, who is studying abroad in Denmark, won her first cross country meet in Sweden! Natalie said: “I love cross country because it really gives you a greater appreciation of sleep”.

Internships!

By: Sabrina Gubbels-Wingo

Post Oak is excited to have a wide variety of partner organizations that offer internship opportunities in several fields. One such organization is Bee2Bee, a beekeeping business that cares for hives all over the Houston area. Post Oak student, Sabrina Guubels-Wingo said, “I was lucky enough to intern with Bee2Bee last school year

and into the summer. Working with Bee2Bee, I was able to learn about both the business and conservation sides to beekeeping. This internship provided me with an outlet to pursue one of my passions, wildlife conservation, while learning about another area that I hadn’t researched before.” If bees and bugs aren’t your thing, we offer a wide selection of internships, or you can suggest your own!

If you would like to start an internship, make sure to contact Ms. Agrawal for more information!
